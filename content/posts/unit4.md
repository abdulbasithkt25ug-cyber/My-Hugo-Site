### Learning Through the Communication Projects

In this unit, I worked on two projects using Typebot and Metabase. This experience was different from normal assignments because there were very few instructions. We had to explore the tools, learn by ourselves, and create something meaningful. At first, this felt difficult because I was not used to learning tools independently.

For the chatbot project, I spent time understanding how Typebot works by watching tutorials and trying different features. In the beginning, I was confused about how to design the conversation flow. Some ideas looked good in my mind but did not work properly when I tested the bot. After trying multiple times, I slowly improved the structure and made the chatbot more clear and interactive.

The dashboard project was also challenging. I learned that data visualization is not only about making charts, but about helping people understand information easily. Choosing the correct graphs and organizing them properly took more time than I expected. Sometimes I created charts that looked confusing, so I had to redesign them again.

One important thing I learned from this unit was the importance of patience and experimentation. Many times, things did not work on the first try. Instead of giving up, I kept testing and improving my work step by step. Discussions with classmates also helped me solve some problems and get new ideas.

Overall, this unit improved my confidence in learning new tools on my own. It also showed me that communication is an important part of Information Science. A good project is not only technically correct, but also easy for others to understand and use.
