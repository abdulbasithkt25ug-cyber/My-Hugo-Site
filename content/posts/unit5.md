### Reflecting on the Smartness Presentation

In Unit 5, our group, Group 6, worked on the topic “Making Sense of Smartness in the Context of Smart Devices and Smart Systems.” This was an interesting experience because it was not only about learning a topic, but also about researching, discussing ideas, and presenting as a team.

At first, I thought smart devices only meant modern technology like smartphones or smartwatches. But while preparing for the presentation, I understood that “smartness” can mean many different things. A device may look smart because it is automated or connected to the internet, but that does not always mean it is truly intelligent. This made me think more critically about technology that we use every day.

One challenge during the preparation was organizing the information clearly. There were many ideas and examples, so we had to decide what was most important for the audience. We also had to make the slides simple and easy to understand instead of adding too much text.

The presentation itself was a valuable experience. I felt nervous before speaking, especially because we had to present confidently without reading from notes. However, after practicing with my group, I became more comfortable. Listening to other groups also helped me understand different social and technological issues related to Information Science.

I also realized the importance of teamwork during this activity. Every member contributed in different ways, such as researching, designing slides, and practicing the presentation. Working together helped us complete the task more effectively.

Overall, this unit improved my communication skills, confidence, and ability to think critically about technology. It showed me that Information Science is not only about technology itself, but also about understanding its impact on society.
