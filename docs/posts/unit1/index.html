Understanding What Information Really Means

Hey I’m Abdul Basith K T studying Information Science in Azim Premji University. In this unit, I learned about what information really means in Information Science. At first, I thought information was just data or facts. But this unit helped me understand that information is more than that. It depends on how we understand and use data.

One thing that changed my thinking was the DIKW model. It showed me that data, information, knowledge, and wisdom are all different. Before this, I used to think they were almost the same. Now I understand that data is just raw facts, and information is when we give meaning to that data.

I also found it interesting that information is not the same for everyone. The same data can give different meaning to different people. This made me realize that understanding depends on the person and the situation. While working with examples like census data, I understood that large amounts of data can be confusing. It is not easy to understand everything at once. This connects with the idea that humans have limits in understanding complex data. So, how we present information is very important.

At first, I felt some confusion because these ideas were new and not like normal subjects. There were no clear right or wrong answers. But slowly, I got used to thinking more deeply and asking questions.

Overall, this unit changed how I look at information. It taught me that learning is not just about collecting data, but about understanding and using it properly. This is an important step for becoming a reflective learner in Information Science.
